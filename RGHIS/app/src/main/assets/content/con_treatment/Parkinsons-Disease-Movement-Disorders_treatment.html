Treatment for Parkinsonв„ўs Disease

Parkinsonв„ўs disease is a slowly progressive disorder. There is currently no cure. However, there are effective medications that can improve the symptoms of the disease. Your doctor will be able to recommend the most appropriate medications depending on your symptoms, age and other medical conditions. Treatment has to be individualised and medications that are suitable for one patient may not be ideal for another. For patients with more advanced disease, limited neurosurgery called Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS) may further improve symptoms. This involves the implantation of a wire into the brain to electrically stimulate that area.

Treatment for Movement Disorders

After the type of abnormal movement has been classified and the cause established, the next step is to reduce the frequency or severity of these movements so as to improve function or reduce discomfort. Oral medications are the main type of treatment used to control these movements. For some of these disorders such as facial spasms or dystonia, botulinum toxin injections may be useful to relieve the symptoms.

In October 2007, our Parkinsonв„ўs Disease and Movement Disorders Centre initiated Singaporeв„ўs first-ever organised outreach programme for the Parkinson community. The NNI-CCPP is a programme that aims to enhance the delivery of community care and support to the Parkinson community by tapping on the well-integrated network of community healthcare facilities in Singapore. Our CCPP Partners help provide the crucial link between NNI and the dependent PD patients in the community. The National Parkinson Foundation

The National Parkinson Foundation (NPF) is the largest and oldest national Parkinson foundation in the United States. NPF supports Parkinson-related research, patient care, education, training, and outreach. The designation of Centre of Excellence constitutes NPFв„ўs public seal of approval, defining the gold standard in Parkinson research, care delivery, and outreach. There are 42 Centres of Excellence in the United States, including 12 in four continents around the world.
