Symptoms

Am I a candidate for Carotid Endarterectomy?

You would be a candidate for surgery if you have experienced any of the following symptoms.

  1. Sudden numbness, weakness or paralysis of the face, arm or leg; especially if this is on one side of the body.
  2. Sudden transient blindness of one eye (amaurosis fugax)
  3. Sudden difficulty talking or understanding speech
  4. These symptoms may last for days, weeks or months. When they disappear completely within 24 hours or less, they are called transient ischaemic attacks (TIAs) or mini-strokes.
